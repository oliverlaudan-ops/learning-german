Greetings & Small Talk

Master everyday greetings and learn how Germans make small talk.

Common Greetings by Time

Morgen! - Morning! (casual, before noon)
Tag! - Hi! (casual, daytime)
Na? - Hey, what's up? (very casual, among friends)
Wie läuft's? - How's it going? (casual)
Alles gut? - Everything good? (casual)

Typical Responses

  • Ja, danke! - Yes, thanks!
  • Passt schon. - It's alright. / No worries.
  • Kann nicht klagen. - Can't complain.
  • So weit, so gut. - So far, so good.
  • Immer noch am Leben! - Still alive! (humorous)

Shopping & Markets

Navigate German shops, markets, and malls with confidence.

Useful Phrases

Was kostet das? - How much is this?
Haben Sie das auch in...? - Do you have this in...?
Darf ich das anprobieren? - May I try this on?
Ich schaue nur. - I'm just looking.
Kann ich mit Karte zahlen? - Can I pay by card?
Ist das im Angebot? - Is this on sale?

At the Supermarket

German supermarkets have some unique features:

  • Bring your own bags (or buy them at checkout)
  • Put your own groceries in bags (cashiers don't do it)
  • Deposit for carts (Pfand) - usually €1-2
  • Separate bottles for Pfand refund

At the Restaurant

Order food like a local and understand German dining customs.

Essential Phrases

Einen Tisch für zwei, bitte. - A table for two, please.
May ich die Speisekarte sehen? - May I see the menu?
Was empfehlen Sie? - What do you recommend?
Ich hätte gerne... - I would like...
Zum Mitnehmen, bitte. - To go, please.
Die Rechnung, bitte. - The bill, please.
Kann ich getrennt zahlen? - Can we pay separately?

Tipping Culture

  • Tipping is expected but not as high as in the US (5-10%)
  • Round up or add a few euros
  • Say the total when paying: "Make it 25" instead of "20 + 5 tip"
  • "Stimmt so" = Keep the change

Asking for Directions

Find your way around German cities and towns.

Key Phrases

Entschuldigung, wo ist...? - Excuse me, where is...?
Wie komme ich zum/zur...? - How do I get to...?
Ist das weit von hier? - Is it far from here?
Können Sie das auf der Karte zeigen? - Can you show that on the map?

Direction Words

geradeaus - straight ahead
links/rechts - left/right
um die Ecke - around the corner
gegenüber - opposite
neben - next to
zwischen - between
hinter - behind
vor - in front of

At the Doctor

Essential phrases for medical appointments and health situations.

Making an Appointment

Ich möchte einen Termin vereinbaren. - I'd like to make an appointment.
Haben Sie heute noch einen Termin frei? - Do you have an appointment available today?
Es ist dringend. - It's urgent.

Describing Symptoms

  • Ich habe Schmerzen. - I have pain.
  • Ich fühle mich schlecht. - I feel sick.
  • Ich habe Fieber. - I have a fever.
  • Ich habe Kopfschmerzen / Bauchschmerzen. - I have a headache / stomachache.

Common Idioms

Sound like a native with these everyday German expressions.

Popular Idioms

  • Das ist mir Wurst. - It's all the same to me. (Literally: "That's sausage to me")
  • Ich verstehe nur Bahnhof. - I don't understand anything. (Literally: "I only understand train station")
  • Da steppt der Bär. - That's where the action is. (Literally: "There dances the bear")
  • Alles in Butter. - Everything's fine. (Literally: "Everything in butter")
  • Die Katze im Sack kaufen. - To buy a pig in a poke. (Literally: "To buy the cat in the bag")
  • Nicht alle Tassen im Schrank haben. - To be a bit crazy. (Literally: "Not all cups in the cupboard")

Cultural Tips

Understand German culture to avoid faux pas and connect with locals.

Important Cultural Norms

  • Punctuality - Being on time means being 5 minutes early
  • Cash is king - Many places still prefer or only accept cash
  • Sunday quiet - Most shops closed, keep noise down
  • Direct communication - Germans are straightforward, not rude
  • Recycling - Take trash separation seriously (Pfand, paper, bio, etc.)
  • Jaywalking - Don't cross on red, even with no cars

Du vs. Sie

German has two forms of "you":

  • Du - Informal (friends, family, peers, children)
  • Sie - Formal (strangers, superiors, older people, professionals)

When in doubt, start with Sie. They'll offer to switch to du if appropriate.
